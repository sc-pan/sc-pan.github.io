About Me

I am a PhD candidate in the Psychology Department at the University of California, San Diego. My research involves learning and memory, with particular emphasis on improving the efficiency and efficacy of educational practice. Current lines of inquiry include (1) the use of retrieval practice to improve memory; (2) investigations of learning in specialized domains such as spelling, and (3) investigating the role of sleep on procedural skills. Previously, I completed my undergraduate studies via the Early Entrance Program at California State University, Los Angeles. My research is currently supported by an NSF Graduate Research Fellowship and previously by an NIMH T34 Pre-Doctoral Ruth L. Kirschstein National Research Service Award.
